Mutton Kunna holds a significant place in Pakistani cuisine, especially in the culinary traditions of rural Punjab. The dish gets its name from the special clay pot, kunna, in which it is cooked. The clay pot not only enhances the flavor but also allows the meat to cook slowly, ensuring that the mutton becomes soft and succulent. The slow-cooking process helps infuse the meat with spices, while the natural clay pot imparts an earthy aroma to the dish. The key ingredients in Mutton Kunna include bone - in mutton, ginger, garlic, yogurt, and an array of spices like cumin, coriander, cardamom, and black pepper.The dish is often garnished with fried onions, green chilies, and fresh coriander.A hallmark of this dish is its rich, velvety gravy, thickened using wheat flour or gram flour, which adds a smooth consistency.Mutton Kunna is traditionally served with naan or roti, making it a hearty and comforting meal.
